If you've ever had a passport application returned because your photo was the
wrong size, you're not alone. The State Department processed over 19 million
US passport applications in 2025 — and a meaningful fraction get bounced for
photo issues.

Here's where the major country rules stand in 2026, distilled into the parts
that actually matter when you're snapping a photo on your phone.

## United States

- **Size:** 2 × 2 inches (600 × 600 px at 300 DPI).
- **Head height:** Between 1 and 1⅜ inches (50-69% of frame).
- **Background:** Plain white or off-white. Backgrounds can be removed
  digitally provided the rest of the photo is unmodified.
- **Glasses:** Not allowed. Removed the formal exemption in 2016; still strict.
- **Expression:** Neutral, both eyes open.
- **Validity:** Photo must be taken within the last 6 months.

The biggest gotcha for 2025-26: many editing apps ship a passport template
that **changes pixel data** — even a smoothing filter. That makes the photo
non-compliant. Our strict mode doesn't touch your pixels: it only crops and
resizes against a white canvas.

## United Kingdom

- **Size:** 35 × 45 mm.
- **Head height:** 32-36 mm (about 70-80% of frame, which is *more* than
  the US expects).
- **Background:** Plain light grey or cream.
- **Glasses:** Allowed for medical reasons; otherwise not.
- **Expression:** Neutral, mouth closed.
- **Validity:** Within the last month.

UK rules are notably tighter on recency. If you're applying in the same
month you took the photo, you're usually fine. If not, retake.

## India

- **Size:** 35 × 35 mm.
- **Head height:** 70-80% of frame.
- **Background:** Plain white.
- **Expression:** Neutral, mouth closed.
- **Eye visibility:** Cropped photos that clip the top of the head are the
  most common rejection reason — keep the head fully in frame.

India is mostly bucketed under "OCI / passport application" rules. The
Indian consulate abroad is more forgiving than the in-country passport
office, which is more forgiving than the eMigrate portal. When in doubt,
follow the strictest version.

## Schengen

- **Size:** 35 × 45 mm.
- **Head height:** 70-80% of frame.
- **Background:** Plain light grey (off-white is also usually accepted).
- **Glasses:** Not allowed (medical exception).
- **Validity:** Within the last 6 months.

Schengen is more forgiving than the US about minor lighting variation, but
will fail you for digital retouching or background manipulation **if it's
visible** in the print.

## How to actually take a usable photo

Three steps, every time:

1. **Lighting.** Use indirect daylight, not flash. Diffuse ceiling lights are
   fine. Avoid shadows across the face.
2. **Framing.** Look straight at the camera. Eyes about 60% up the frame.
3. **Background.** Stand against a plain wall. If you don't have one, our
   "Relaxed" mode will swap the background for you digitally.

When in doubt, use the strict mode in [our passport tool](/tools/passport-photo-maker).
It does no digital retouching beyond crop, white-fill, and resize — which
is what the rules are reluctant to forgive.
