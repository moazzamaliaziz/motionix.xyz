A question we get a lot: how can you offer these tools for free? The short
answer is that most of the work happens on your device, not on ours. The
long answer is more interesting — and useful for any solo founder figuring
out where to spend their hosting budget.

## The cost floor we're working with

Our entire production environment breaks down into one line item: a server
that costs roughly $20/month. That's it. We don't pay S3 egress, we don't
pay a worker fleet, we don't pay for GPUs. We do pay for a domain, and a
modest amount for analytics that we'd skip if push came to shove.

The reason this works: nearly everything happens on the visitor's device.

## What does happen on our server

Three things, and only three:

1. **Statics.** The Next.js bundle, OG images, the sitemap, robots.txt.
   Vercel's free tier covers this for any volume we'd see in year one.
2. **A tiny history endpoint.** When you're signed in, your saved work is
   persisted to MongoDB Atlas. Free tier gives us 512 MB of storage.
3. **Cloudflare R2 for opt-in cloud upload.** Storage is $0.015/GB/month
   and egress is zero — which means we make money on cost savings over S3.

In practice, that means our cost-per-user per-month is in the fractions of
a cent. Even at six-figure MAU we'd be paying for a slightly bigger
server.

## What the server does NOT do

This is the list that earns us the cost profile:

- **No image processing on a worker.** We use Canvas, ONNX, UPNG, and
  WebCodecs.
- **No model inference.** The largest model (background removal) is shipped
  through the browser's HTTP cache, downloaded once per device.
- **No video transcoding.** WebCodecs handles it.
- **No file storage by default.** Cloud upload is opt-in. Saved work lives
  on the user's device unless they ask for a cloud link.

If a feature needs a server round-trip, we look first for a way to do it
on the user's device. Failing that, we look for a way to do it on Vercel's
free tier.

## When we'll start paying real money

We're not purists about it. If a feature requires server work, we'll do
server work. The list of features that will require server hours:

- **Server-side video transcoding** for files over 200 MB or in obscure
  codecs.
- **Print-on-demand** for passport photos that need real-photographic-grade
  output.
- **Team / shared history** for users who want collaborative storage.

When those features ship, the cost tells us when to introduce a paid tier
or expand paid bundles. The unit economics of tools 1-8 are basically
free, so it doesn't matter if everyone in the world uses them.

## Why this matters for your project

If you're a solo founder building something that runs on someone else's
data, designing for browser-first isn't just a privacy decision — it's
a runway decision. We ran our first six months without anyone paying us
a cent and never came close to running out of hosting budget.

That's the bedrock. Everything else — design, content, distribution — is
uphill from there.
